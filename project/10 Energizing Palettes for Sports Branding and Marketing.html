10 Energizing Color Palettes for Sports Branding and Marketing

By Grace Fussell on August 5, 2021
Grace Fussell
Grace is a graphic designer and design writer based in Manchester in the UK. A self-confessed 'print geek' with an enthusiasm for all things related to print design and branding, Grace heads up creative agency Blue Whippet Studio and runs the design blog InDesignSkills.com.

Ten winning color palettes for sports branding, including a contemporary scheme for tennis brands and a classic Olympics-inspired palette.

With the ability to mentally and physically energize the viewer, color can be a powerful marketing tool for sports design and branding. An intelligent use of color can also be specifically tailored to different sports and products. Take, for example, high-octane acid neons for soccer branding, or serene and soothing tones for yoga marketing.  

Read on to discover ten winning color palettes for sports branding inspired by current marketing trends. Once you’ve found the perfect scheme for your next sports-themed project, why not pin palettes to a Pinterest board or moodboard, or share them with colleagues?

Scroll down to discover color palettes that will get you and your designs energized and motivated! 

How to Use Your Sports Branding Palettes

Perhaps you’re creating a brand identity for a gym, a new product line for surfers, or signage designs for a sporting event. Color plays a vital role in attracting the eye of your target consumer or viewer, as well as making them feel energized and excited about the product or event. 

Particular colors have been proven to raise heart rates (red), boost metabolism (orange), or make an individual feel more optimistic about an upcoming competition or game (yellow). You can wield some of these psychological powers in your own designs through clever use of color combinations.

To create these palettes, we’ve researched the sports marketing trends that are making waves right now across large sporting corporates such as Nike, Adidas, and Under Armour, as well as across major sports federations and events including ESPN, Sky Sports, the International Olympic Committee (IOC), and the Premier League. 

The ten palettes below can be used to give your designs a particular emotional impact or can be used individually to target particular areas of sports branding. For example, tropical hues are perfect for surfing products, while an urban-inspired scheme that would make a likely fit for boxing or gym branding.   

Palette 1: Euro Classic

Inspired by the brand redesign for Eurosport by Pentagram, this palette combines classic navy and gray with contemporary coral red and fresh yolk yellow. This combination of traditional sporting colors with fresher hues creates a balanced scheme that would be a great all-round choice for sports events or broadcaster branding.

This scheme offers an updated take on traditional navy and red, which is a commonly used color pairing historically across a wide variety of sports.

For sports branding that requires a slightly more conservative approach, perhaps for sports such as golf, cricket, or rugby, this palette creates the perfect harmony between old and new.  

Palette 2: Neon Energy

Neon colors are often used in sports marketing, and for good reason. Energizing, zesty, and high-octane, these types of colors are highly-stimulating for viewers, and are particularly attention-grabbing on light-emitting screens, including computers and televisions.

This palette combines three acid neon colors from the cooler end of the spectrum with deep, rich purple. Purple anchors the scheme, making it feel mature and grounded—ideal for projects that need both energy and a corporate element, such as branding for soccer, basketball, or baseball. 

English soccer league Premier League rebranded their marketing communications with a similar palette of neon tones and deep purple, striking the balance between high-octane energy and grounded heritage.

Palette 3: Competitive Spirit

With a German study concluding that English soccer teams who played in red kits won around 10% more games, there’s now a scientifically-proven reason why red is a go-to color for team kits across a range of sports.

Assertive, bold, and sure to get the blood pumping, red is a competitive color that can be used just as effectively across sports branding as it is on soccer shirts. 

This color palette combines red with monochrome tones, allowing this confident hue to really take centerstage. Perfect for team branding, such as crests, logos, and banners, as well as sports uniforms, this is a scheme that ensures you’ll be in first place . . . at least 10% of the time. 

Palette 4: Urban Workout

If you’re creating a brand identity or merchandise for a sports venue or business in a city setting, this urban-infused palette will help to inject your project with both energy and edge. Florescent green brings an enlivening feel to an otherwise monochrome palette, while black and gray provide a cool, crisp backdrop. 

Neon green and black are an ultra high-contrast pairing, making this palette particularly effective for designs that need to catch the attention of passers-by, such as web banners, posters, or signage. 

Keep in mind, neon colors will need to be printed using spot color swatches to maximize their impact.  

Palette 5: Inclusive International

Inspired by the world-recognized color scheme of the Olympic rings, this multi-colored scheme is instinctually evocative of international sporting events.

This multi-colored palette can symbolically indicate inclusion or global participation, making it an excellent choice for large sports events or broadcasters who are showcasing sports content from around the world. 

Primary brights in sunshine shades will bring an optimistic mood to any project. A series of equally-balanced colors can also be helpful for theming different sports across a broader event, making this palette a hard-working, versatile choice for multi-sport events.

Palette 6: Serene Spectrum

Lavender might not be the color that immediately springs to mind when it comes to sports, but it makes a fresh and relevant choice for more serene and soothing sports such as yoga or pilates. 

When combined with violet and earth tones, pastel purple looks chic and contemporary. Brown and greige grounds the scheme, making this a palette that combines airy, invigorating colors with an earthy base.

Nike used a similar scheme in their recent identity and apparel for Nike Yoga. But, these fresh, soothing colors would also be a good fit for other sports connected with mental wellbeing and control, such as dance, martial arts, or wild swimming.

Palette 7: Teal Refresh

Invigorating and refreshing, this elemental palette combines nature-inspired colors to evoke a sense of poolside activity or snow-strewn slopes.

With stylish teal and rust orange at its center, this outdoorsy scheme would make a perfect partner for branding for aquatic sports such as diving and swimming, or alternatively winter sports, such as skiing or snowboarding.  

Cool and fresh with a slightly retro feel, this color scheme is a good alternative sports palette for designers looking for something a little different to the norm. 

Palette 8: Retro Race Track

Sometimes, you can’t beat a classic. This retro-infused palette looks to the colors used in sports in decades past, combining solid navy blue with Seventies-inspired mustard yellow, cherry red, and Mid-Century sky blue. 

This is a good scheme to try for brands that want to look more established or formal, while retaining a sense of fun. Sports with a treasured history will also find this type of scheme works well to tap into nostalgia marketing.

Try using this retro palette for motor sports, football, or rugby. 

Palette 9: Surf’s Up

Surfing has always been a fringe sport with its own close-knit community and unique identity, with the sport only being recognized as an Olympic sport for Tokyo 2020 (now 2021, of course).

Tropical tones of deep coral, aqua green, sunshine yellow, and marine blue help to keep a surf-themed design looking suitably unique and beach-appropriate.

Use these sunshine hues in combination for surfing products or retail stores, or combine the colors into gradient swatches for sunset-inspired style.

Palette 10: Tennis Clay

Tennis can have a stiflingly traditional reputation (think tennis whites and lawn courts). But, in recent years, the tennis marketing industry has shifted towards more contemporary takes on branding to appeal to younger and more diverse audiences.

With this in mind, this tennis-themed palette offers a fresh take on a tennis color scheme. The acid green of tennis balls is, of course, included, but this feels edgier when teamed with gray, black, and rich clay red. 

This color palette touches on some of the core colors that make tennis distinctive, but replacing white and lawn green with crisp gray and black help to give it a more contemporary flavor.

Try using this scheme for club logos, marketing posters, or social media posts, to give your tennis designs a stylish edge.
